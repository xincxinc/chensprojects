LISTENING TO MUSIC IN THE TIME OF CLOUD

PROJECT 10

Project 10 is a small show. It indicates that individuals' perception in music cannot be totally generalized by machine. Digital music platforms are using intelligent recommendation systems to help us consume. They can give us advices but we shouldn‘t let algorithms do all the choices.

2019 | KIEL | BACHELOR PROJECT

#Design Research #Speculative Design #Interaction Design

BACKGROUND

Choices, Consumerism and AI

In the Digital age, music consumption has been gradually shifting to Online. Vast amounts of music are stored in the cloud platforms and become streaming music. Faced with numerous choices, companies are collecting Big Data and using algorithm to help listeners choose quickly and consume efficiently. They’ve curated different playlists like “music for afternoon” or “customized daily mix”. However, can people’s tastes of music be determined entirely by computer algorithms?

RESEARCH

Transition of Listening Behaviour

At the beginning of the project I conducted 9 interviews with students, office workers, music professional to get to know how people listen to music today and their attitudes towards recommendation system of streaming platform. The results are shown below:

Most people listen to music frequently and think the recommendation algorithm is a good thing.
QUOTE:“I listen to music all the time.” “I love music. I can’t live without music.” “The recommendation system is really good.” “Algorithm is more professional.” ......

To have an insight into the trend, I compared the results with a study. The study conducted in 2006 by Emap*, identified four main types of music listeners. Also based on this study, Paul Lamere** has estimated the number of interactions, on average, a listener of a given type would tolerate to create a listening session.
*Emap is a UK-based advertising agency. **Lamere is former director of developer platforms at The Echo Nest/Spotify.

Mechanism of Recommendation Algorithms

After some secondary research about several popular digital platforms of streaming music, I visualised the mechanism of Spotify's Taste Profile - the core of its recommendation system - according to Musica Analytica: The Datafication of Listeningby Robert Prey, 2016.

Algorithms do konw people to some extend. However their most important role is to work for companies' interests. They only offer individuals generalized answers.

OUTCOME

A show presenting (A)human's unpredictable perception while listening to music and (B)the limitation of machine(digital platform)'s perception

DESIGN MY TOOLS

To collect people's perception in music I designed 2 tools.

1. Interaction System

Four basic parameters of music were set corresponding to different visual feedbacks, through which people can describe or express their feelings about music.

2. Dial Box

With the dial box people can use the interaction system. The dial box is a tool with which people speak out their opinions.
