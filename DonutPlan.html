#Design Research #User Interface #User Experience

If you search "fitness" in the app store, you'll find many apps with...

  • "muscles"
  • "perfect shapes"
  • "training plans"
  • "challenges"

Here's some hard facts:

According to WHO, 39% of adults aged 18 years and over were overweight in 2016, and 13% were obese.
Accoring to a fitness model: "I probably exercised more than an hour per day 6-7 days per week."

Please allow me to translate these words:

Many people's figures are not even close to the fitness model.
If you don't exercise that hard, you'll never be like them.

As a result...

  • "I couldn't finish a workout. I felt miserable and gave it up."
  • "I had workout plans. It always works well at first. But sometimes I'm busy or just tired. I passed one session, then I passed two..."
  • "I don't have stong willpower to train myself at home."
  • "It's tiring. I can find a thousand reasons not to do the exercise."

"Donut Plan" provides a new perspective of fitness training.

No Plan, Smart Arrangement

Workouts are showed in modules. Every module combines workouts with different length and focus. No need to filter and choose a workout everytime. No Need to obey a strict plan.

Food as Motivation

Your Training Goals are never so reachable. See how much effort you should make to burn the calories of a Donut.

Share What You've Gained

Tell your friend how many calories you've burned. And maybe it's time for another beer. Invite your friend to have a beer in a new way. :D

- RESEARCH -

Interviews & Affinity Mapping

I interviewed some people near me in order to know about their experience of doing workout. Most of them are students or office workers. Some have pretty much experience while some very little.

Finding

- Experienced people would rather do training in a gym, unless there's no affordable gym nearby.
- Starters or students with low budget or people with very inactive lifestyle would like to use workout app.
- People don't like too many choices of workouts which make it difficult to start.
- People don't like to receive notifications. They either have fixed plans (eg. doing yoga at 4 p.m. every Saturday) or need very flexible plans.

Personas

I synthesized the interviews and built three personas.
